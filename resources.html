Other Resources

Books

The book that helped me learn what I currently know is Hacking: The Art of Exploitation by Jon Erickson. The book dives straight into the C programming language and a light introduction to x86 assembly. If you have no prior programming experience, this book will be a very tough read. However if you stick with it you will learn a lot. I recommend downloading the ISO of the CD that comes with the book and trying all of the exercieses within a Virtual Machine. This will help you cemement what you read in the book, into your head.

YouTube Channels

If you are more of a visual learner, there are a multitude of YouTube channels that can assist your learning. One of my favorites for this is LiveOverflow. His earlier videos are introductions to binary exploitaiton and walk throughs of CTFs while his newer work is a little more advanced. However even his newer more advanced material is very well explained and benefitial.

Another solid channel is the Cyber Mentor. His content is more focused at those interested in gaining a job in IT Security/Pentesting.
